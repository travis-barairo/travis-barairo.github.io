Projects

Women's Clothing Amazon Sales

Using a open-use data set on Kaggle entitled "Amazon Reviews on Women Dresses," I cleaned the 20,000 plus rows and 11 columns by removing the null values, verifying data types, and transforming the dataframe such that I had the necessary columns to solve three key business questions: What class of clothing would most likely be recommended again by customers? What is the age demographic for customers buying our clothes categorized by the apparel's classification? Lastly, we wanted to know the breakdown of which clothes are most bought in the store by department. Looking at the visualization above, we can see that Dresses, Knits, and Blouses are most likely to be recommended again by customers, and that across all classifications of clothing our main demographic is women ages 30-49. Lastly by breakdown, most of the clothing purchased in the store is that of tops and dresses which gives us insight as to what our company should be stocking more of. Imagine Airy Electronics Sales Data Analysis

This project's primary focus was the aggregating of 12 months of sales data for an imaginary electronics company looking to discover ways of making advertising more effective and generating more sales. To achieve this, we took a look at a few key metrics such as, monthly sales patterns, hourly ordering volume, aggregated sales by city state, most ordered electronic pairs, and the top grossing products by order volume and revenue. Follow this link to find follow the github repository where you'll find the README.md file containing all the steps for analysis!

Cyclistic Trip Data Analysis

In this project, the key business task at hand was to determine the differences between casual users of the Cyclistic bike share platform and membership users. The insights and findings from this exercise would be used to drive future marketing and influencing campaigns in the attempt to increase casual to membership conversion. Click this link to view the github repository and read more!

About

My name is Travis Barairo. I graduated with the highest honors from California State University East Bay, with a Bachelor's of Science and Engineering in Construction Management. I am in the process of making a career transition from a Civil Engineering role to a position in Data Analytics.

I found my love for analytics in my most recent position as a Cost Manager. While I worked in this position I was assigned to do a revenue analysis for the company. Altough it was hard, and at the time I was still unfamiliar with the concept of storytelling with data, this exercise allowed me to explore a new direction which I ended up finding enthralling.

I took the initiative and worked hard to complete my Google Data Analytics Certification within 2 months. During this period I also learned how to program with SQL to query from the databases, learned Python for use in cleaning and analyzing data, turned raw data into beautiful visualizations using Tableau, and taught myself foundational HTML programming for fun!

If I were to describe my strongest personality trait, it'd have to be that I am ambitious and willing to put in extra effort to get the job done. I enjoy working with others towards a large goal, and find myself losing track of time when I get started working on an analysis project.

I aim to one day be a director or data executive such that I can help others similar to myself in finding opportunities to grow and develop.
